Leopard

The unofficial non-identical twin of the cheetah, the leopard is also part of the cat family. Although they are endangered in some parts of the world, we still have a very large leopard population in Sub-Sharan Africa. But don't expect to see one of these spotty creatures on a midday game drive, as they are nocturnal and prefer to come out and hunt at night. Differences between leopards and cheetahs

  1. Cheetahs have a black line running down from both eyes
  2. Leopards' spots are called rossettes, as they look like small roses, while a cheetah has normal spots
  3. Cheetahs usually hunt during the day, while leopardsn hunt bat night
  4. Cheetahs are generally faster than leopards
